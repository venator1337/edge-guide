Getting better results
Good habits for better prompts

The more specific your prompt, the more useful the response. Vague questions tend to get vague answers. Try framing your requests like these:

Summarise "Summarise this page in three bullet points for a non-technical audience."
Draft "Turn these notes into a short professional email to my manager."
Explain "Explain what this term means in simple language."
Focus "What are the key risks mentioned on this page? List them only."

Asking for a specific format — such as "three bullet points" or "one short paragraph" — and being clear about the intended outcome tends to produce much more useful responses than open-ended questions.
