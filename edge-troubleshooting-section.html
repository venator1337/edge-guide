Troubleshooting

Edge not behaving?
Try these steps first.

Before raising a support request, most Edge problems can be resolved in a few minutes. Work through the steps below — they fix the vast majority of common issues.

Start here
Try these fixes in order

Check for updates

An out-of-date browser is one of the most common causes of loading errors and instability. Head to Settings → Help and feedback → About Microsoft Edge — Edge will check for updates automatically and install them.

Settings → Help & feedback → About Microsoft Edge

Restart Edge — or your computer

Close the browser completely (not just the window) and reopen it. If the issue persists, a full computer restart clears temporary problems that a browser restart alone can't fix.

Clear your browsing data

If pages are loading incorrectly, showing outdated content, or behaving unexpectedly, clearing your cache and cookies is often the fix. This does not delete your bookmarks or passwords.

Settings → Privacy, search & services → Clear browsing data

Disable extensions temporarily

If a problem appeared suddenly without any other changes, an extension is a likely culprit. Try disabling your extensions one by one to identify the cause — or open an InPrivate window, which runs without extensions by default.

Settings → Extensions — or press Ctrl+Shift+N for InPrivate
Still stuck?
When to contact the Service Desk

If you've worked through the steps above and the problem continues, it's time to raise a support request. Contact the Service Desk if any of the following apply to you.

Edge still won't open after restarting your computer
Work websites consistently fail or won't load in Edge
Your bookmarks or browser data didn't transfer correctly
The issue is preventing you from doing your work

Contact the Service Desk

Use your usual support channel — details are in your team's IT contact page.
